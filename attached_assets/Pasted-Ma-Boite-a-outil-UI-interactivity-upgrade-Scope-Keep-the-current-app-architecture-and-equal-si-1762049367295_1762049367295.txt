Ma Boite a outil — UI & interactivity upgrade

Scope

Keep the current app architecture and equal-size tiles on the home page.

Work ONLY on the flashcard experience and examples inside each card.

Do not remove the word-replacement feature; expand and polish it.

1) Images in every example

For every example sentence, include a small illustration that depicts the sentence (joyful, classroom-friendly, same visual style across the app).

Image placement: left of the sentence on mobile (stacked), and a two-column layout on tablet/desktop so images and sentences sit side-by-side.

Rounded corners, soft shadow, and alt text that describes the scene (“Garçon avec un ballon”, “Astronaute se prépare”, etc.).

Reuse my slide visuals as stylistic references (same vibe, colors, and clarity).

2) Color coding like the slides

Color the target word in the sentence using the same scheme as the images:

Verbe → green

Déterminant → orange

Pronom → blue

Conjonction → red

Adverbe → yellow

Préposition → purple

The colored word remains bold, readable, and has a dotted underline to signal interactivity.

Show a tiny category label or icon under the sentence (“C’est un déterminant”, “C’est le verbe être”, etc.), matching the color.

3) Bigger, kid-friendly typography

Flashcard title: noticeably larger than now (desktop ≈ 32–40 px; mobile ≈ 22–28 px).

Section titles (“Règle”, “Exemples”, “Exercices”): desktop ≈ 24–28 px; mobile ≈ 18–22 px.

Body text: desktop ≈ 18–20 px; mobile ≈ 16–18 px. Tight line spacing (snug but not cramped).

Buttons are large with generous padding and clear labels; primary actions are obvious.

4) Flashcard modal ergonomics

Modal fills about 90% of the screen on laptop/desktop; only the content area scrolls.

Keep a sticky header (title + navigation + close) and a sticky footer (audio + “Suivant” or “Marquer comme complété”).

Add a small legend below “Exemples”: “Clique sur les mots soulignés pour tester un remplacement.”

5) Word-replacement interaction (expand)

Words with replacements are underlined and colored. On click:

Show “Essayer avec « … »”, then temporarily swap only that word, highlight the swapped token, and show the short hint “✅ Remplacement possible — bon indice.”

“Revenir à la phrase” brings back the original.

Each occurrence is clickable independently; whole-word matches only.

Keep the existing pairs and ensure they’re present across cards (e.g., est→était, es→étais, sont→étaient, a→avait, ou→ou bien, où→dans quel endroit, ça→cela, sa→ma, son→mon, ce→le, se→me, plus an explanation bubble for ces/ses/c’est/s’est/sais/sait).

6) Make the flashcards more interactive

Add a “Découvrir” micro-interaction in the Rule section (tap to reveal a short tip or counter-example).

Add a tiny progress chip at the top (“Carte 3 / 8”) and confetti or stars on a perfect score.

7) More exercises per card

Each flashcard should include at least 5 short exercises (varied formats), with instant feedback:

QCM (2–3 quick items).

Compléter la phrase: choose the correct homophone.

Glisser-déposer: associer mot → catégorie (verbe/pronom/…).

Relier: phrase ↔ remplacement correct (ex.: son ↔ mon).

Trouve l’intrus: une option n’appartient pas à la catégorie.

Dictée courte (audio) avec un blanc à remplir par l’élève.

After submission, show a score for the card and allow “Refaire” to practice again.

8) Audio polish

Keep play/pause + 0.8× / 1× / 1.2×.

While audio plays, lightly highlight the current sentence. Prevent double-taps from restarting unexpectedly.

9) Accessibility & performance

Clear focus styles and keyboard shortcuts (Esc closes; ←/→ navigate; Space toggles audio).

Good contrast for text on tinted backgrounds.

Optimized images (fast load, no layout shifts) and descriptive alt text.

Acceptance checks

Every example has its own image, with alt text and consistent style.

Colored, underlined target words are visible and clickable; replacement toggle works for each occurrence.

Fonts are significantly larger than the current screenshot and remain readable on mobile.

Each card includes ≥ 5 interactive exercises with instant feedback and a per-card score.

The flashcard modal occupies ~90% of the viewport; only the inner content scrolls.

Home page tiles stay equal size across categories.
